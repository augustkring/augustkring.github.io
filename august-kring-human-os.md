## My Beliefs

What I stand for.

### 1. Impact

**Core Belief:** People, planet, and profit belong in one equation. Not three.

The best products and partnerships are the ones where making money and making a difference reinforce each other. Not as a nice idea. As a design principle.

Every system, every collaboration should produce something that holds up humanly, environmentally, and financially. AI makes this more possible than ever — if we use it to extend how we think and create, not just how we automate.

Co-creation over own-creation. Shared knowledge over hoarded advantage.

### 2. Mindset

**Core Belief:** Learn. Unlearn. Relearn. That is the only mindset that holds.

Mindset is everything. In a world where AI is reshaping industries, new ways of working appear overnight, and no one can predict what comes next. The way people think decides whether they adapt or get left behind.

That means building mindsets — across people, organizations, and AI systems — that can learn, unlearn, and relearn with minimal friction. When that foundation is shared, real co-creation becomes possible. Without it, nothing holds.

### 3. Execution

**Core Belief:** Strategy without execution is just a story you tell yourself.

Think big, start small, scale fast. Always in that order.

Build the smallest thing that proves it works. Scale what gets traction. Kill what doesn't. Stay capital efficient — not spending more, but spending right.

With AI in the mix, execution speed is the real edge. Clear standards for how humans and AI contribute together is what turns ideas into products and revenue.

Execution is not the follow-up to strategy. It is the strategy.

### 4. Relationships

**Core Belief:** What you build is only as strong as who you build it with.

Once you know how you are wired, you can find the people who complement it. The right alignment brings trust, perspective, and access to opportunities that do not exist in isolation.

But it is not just about fit. Building a strong network — and showing up with directness, commitment, and honesty — is what opens the door to creating something far greater than anything you could build on your own. That is the real leverage.

## Personality Types

The frameworks behind how I operate.

### MBTI: ENTP-Turbulent

Wired to challenge assumptions and find better ways to do things.

**What it means:**
MBTI maps how people take in information and make decisions. ENTP describes someone energised by ideas, patterns, and exploration. Extraverted (64%), Intuitive (51%), Thinking (79%), Prospecting (68%). The Turbulent modifier adds a layer of internal self-scrutiny and sensitivity that does not always show on the surface.

**How it shows up in my work:**
A natural drive to challenge assumptions, explore better ways of doing things, and connect ideas across domains. I am more interested in what could be built next than in maintaining what already exists. I think fast, see patterns early, and move toward innovation, strategy, and future-facing opportunity. I am comfortable going fast before the full picture is clear.

**Strengths it gives me:**
Energy around change, experimentation, and new ideas. Comfort in ambiguity. A natural ability to reframe problems, generate alternative approaches, and keep momentum alive in situations where others slow down.

**Watch-outs:**
Impatience with repetition. A pull toward what is new over what needs finishing. The Turbulent side creates internal pressure and self-scrutiny when something matters deeply, even when that is not visible from the outside.

### Enneagram: Type 7 with 8 — SX/SO

Driven by momentum, freedom, and the need to make something happen.

**What it means:**
The Enneagram maps core motivation. Type 7 is driven by possibility, freedom, and the desire to avoid feeling trapped or limited. The 8 wing adds directness and a stronger instinct to take charge. SX/SO stacking intensifies everything; it creates a drive for deep engagement and a pull toward meaningful, visible impact.

**How it shows up in my work:**
Strong forward-moving energy with intensity behind it. I am drawn to opportunity, momentum, and expansion, and I bring boldness and initiative into how I think and build. When things move, I thrive. When things stall, I look for a way to restart. The 8 wing means I am willing to push and create friction when that is what progress requires.

**Strengths it gives me:**
Entrepreneurial energy, resilience, and the ability to create momentum in uncertain situations. Deep engagement with what matters. I hold many threads at once without becoming paralysed, and I keep energy alive where others slow down.

**Watch-outs:**
Moving on too fast. The intensity of SX-first can overwhelm people or situations that need a softer approach. Chasing too many directions at once. The desire to stay stimulated can work against staying long enough to fully finish what I start.

### BG5: Express Builder

Best when shaping ideas into something others can see and respond to.

**What it means:**
BG5 is the business application of Human Design. The Express Builder is designed to create through energy, expression, and visible movement. The key principle is response over initiation; when there is something real to engage with, the energy is powerful and sustained. When forced to generate from nothing, it fades.

**How it shows up in my work:**
I do my best work when I can take ideas, shape them into concepts, and move them toward something others can see, use, or respond to. Projects with visible momentum and real engagement go better than projects that start from pure planning without contact with reality. I create faster when I am responding to something real, not starting from a blank page.

**Strengths it gives me:**
Visible creation, entrepreneurial movement, and a natural connection between energy and practical output. A strong bias toward making things real rather than keeping them abstract. Speed and sustained energy when the work has something tangible to respond to.

**Watch-outs:**
Creating faster than I refine. Moving with so much energy that surrounding structures struggle to keep up. It can also be hard to sustain momentum on projects where the response trigger is missing or the direction is unclear.

### Human Design: Manifesting Generator (2/4)

Built to move fast across multiple lanes without needing a linear path.

**What it means:**
Human Design maps how a person is designed to operate, make decisions, and interact with the world. A Manifesting Generator has both the capacity to initiate and the ability to build sustainably. Unlike pure Generators, they can skip steps, move faster, and work across multiple lanes at once. The 2/4 profile adds natural talent and a strong relational network.

**How it shows up in my work:**
Strong creative energy, fast iteration, and a natural tendency to move between ideas, systems, and opportunities without needing a perfectly linear path. My four defined channels reflect this: Curiosity (the searcher), Charisma (awareness becoming action), Recognition (depth of feeling as fuel), and Synthesis (sensitivity to what a community needs). I build across multiple layers at once, often faster than the structures around me expect.

**Strengths it gives me:**
Speed, adaptability, and the ability to carry energy across more territory than most. A natural instinct for finding shorter paths through complexity. The 2/4 profile means I attract the right people and opportunities through presence rather than pursuit.

**Watch-outs:**
This kind of energy can look scattered from the outside if it is not grounded in clear structure and direction. The non-linear way I work can frustrate people or systems that expect straight-line progress. Speed without structure creates noise.

### Understand Myself: High Openness, High Extraversion, Low Agreeableness

High originality, high intensity, low interest in consensus for its own sake.

**What it means:**
Understand Myself is a trait-based psychological assessment built on the Big Five model. My profile shows very high openness (96) and extraversion (91), high conscientiousness (78), very low agreeableness (2), and moderate neuroticism (61). This points to someone who is idea-driven, outward-moving, intense, and comfortable thinking differently.

**How it shows up in my work:**
A mix of creativity, strategic thinking, and intensity. High openness supports innovation and conceptual thinking. High extraversion supports communication, momentum, and outward-facing energy. High conscientiousness keeps me committed when the direction matters. Very low agreeableness means I will challenge weak reasoning and hold my position. That is a strength, but it can also come across harder than intended.

**Strengths it gives me:**
Originality, initiative, vision, communication, and the willingness to think differently and move first. A natural comfort with going against consensus when the reasoning supports it. Strong creative and strategic output under pressure.

**Watch-outs:**
High openness can become distraction. Low agreeableness can create relational friction when directness is not matched with enough context or care. Moderate neuroticism creates internal pressure and overthinking when the stakes feel personal, even when that is not visible externally.

## My Story

How I got here.

### Early Years: My Dad Was Always Creating

My parents split before I was born. Grew up in Slagelse. At six, my dad and uncle moved to Copenhagen to start a business. My mom and I followed. Ideas, ventures, futuristic goals. That was home. My dad later met my stepmother, and I got two younger sisters. I did not know the word entrepreneur yet. I just thought that was how everyone lived.

### School Years: The Classroom Never Had a Chance

Started at Rudolf Steiner, a creativity-focused school that fit how I think. By the later years I was writing papers on exponential technological change and market validation while building a business with a friend after school. We got far. Never launched. Boarding school in 9th grade was about personal growth, not grades. Then Niels Brock Innovationsgymnasium. Three months in, the school fatigue won. I sat in class building another business. Never launched either. I could not stop creating. School was never going to be the place for it.

### Limitless VR: Someone Finally Let Me Loose

Full-time at Limitless VR. Floor to assistant manager to manager, fast. Team of fifteen. Denmark's biggest VR venue. Then they let me build. Loyalty programmes. B2B rental. Event concepts. Real projects that shipped and made money. First time something I created hit the real world and worked. That confirmed it. Execution is how I learn. Get me close to something real and let me figure it out.

### Burnout: The Bill Came Due

No structure at work. None in my personal life. Too much on my shoulders, and things from my upbringing I had never dealt with. It ended in a burnout that took two years. I moved to Faaborg to be close to family.

### Rebuild: Rebuilt From the Studs

Those two years taught me more than everything before. I learned my limits for real. Rebuilt physically, mentally, personally, spiritually. Mindset became a daily practice. My faith deepened. I got back to work in a structured environment and earned responsibility again. This time with ground underneath me. And a clarity that stability alone would never be enough.

### Today: The Pull to Build Came Back

The need to create returned. Clearer than before. KRING, the family business, was restructuring and building a new AI-native platform with venture solutions across different concepts. Something clicked. AI is not about productivity. It is about how we think, work, and create value together. That is what I am building now.
