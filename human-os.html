The pain

Nobody has a real map of how you actually work.

Not your next employer. Not your AI. Sometimes not even you. The cost shows up in wrong roles, broken partnerships, teams that don't click, and AI that forgets you exist.

The résumé lies by omission

It describes jobs, not wiring. Hiring on it is a coin flip dressed up as a process — and the six months of "cultural onboarding" is the cost of that flip landing wrong.

Your AI resets you every session

You re-explain who you are into every new prompt. It flatters more than it coaches. It agrees more than it balances. You get efficiency, not judgment.

You guess at yourself, too

Without a framework, self-knowledge stays fragmented. You know you in pieces — a Myers-Briggs letter, a founder story, a vague sense of "I'm like this." Never one picture.

How it works

One profile. Three layers. Read in order.

The order matters. Wiring is your starting point. Experience is what you've done with it. Mindset is how far you can go from here. Read any other way and the picture breaks.

01
Layer 01 · Personality

How you're wired.

Your operating system. The part that rarely changes. Big Five for the science, Enneagram for the motivation, MBTI for the cognition, Human Design or BG5 if they resonate. Four lenses, one picture.

02
Layer 02 · Experience

What you've built.

The pattern behind your track record. Not titles — what you've shipped, led, and survived. Experience either confirms your wiring, or shows where it got suppressed.

03
Layer 03 · Mindset

Where you're going.

What you believe. What you stand for. The direction you're moving. The part that decides whether you can learn, unlearn, and relearn as the world shifts.

"Four lenses, one picture."
